NAU IEEE Student Branch

Welcome

Welcome to the Northern Arizona University IEEE Student Branch page. We represent you, the students, and faculty in Electrical Engineering. Not an EE? No problem! If you have an interest in electrical engineering, stop on by! We offer homework help, snacks for sale, and a place to execute your ideas. Members can be found in the Engineering Building, Room 237 also referred to as the Electrical Engineering Capstone Room.

Space for Projects

The IEEE club offers access to a few soldering irons, Arduinos and Rasberry Pis for your own personal projects. We also encourage members to present on any of their projects at meetings.

Events

Soldering Workshops

Typically held once a semester, come by and visit our soldering workshops to practice your skills or even learn to solder for the first time.

IEEE Hackathon

Stay tuned for our first hackathon. The competition is open to all NAU students and the prompt will be announced at the beginning of the event. All materials will be suplied to you. We encourage teams of three of people comfortable with coding, soldering, and problem solving.

Club Trips

Hackathons

Let your officers know if there is a hackathon coming up that you'd like to participate in!

IEEE Phoenix Banquet

Keep your ears open for our annual IEEE banquet where the club goes down to the valley to meet with the Phoenix chapter. Enjoy free food and and presentations. This event usually takes place near the beginning of the Spring semester.

Hungry?

Our chapter offers a snack store that is often stocked with food and drinks priced for under $5. Come by Room 237 to find Redbull, chimichangas, and other snacks and drinks. We accept card via SquarePay and cash. For any suggestions, email Kristen at kks99@nau.edu

Lockers

Want a locker in the engineering building? Contact James atjjs338@nau.edu Locker rentals are currently going for $5 per year.
